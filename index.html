The Story

Out of Slapout

From the Montgomery, Alabama area — Slapout, the stretch of red dirt and river road Nashville drives past without slowing down. Not the postcard South. The other one. Gas stations where somebody is always waiting on somebody who is already late.

The first few seconds might fool you. A slide guitar. A gospel voice. A country line. Then the floor drops. This is poverty, paranoia, dope smoke, backroad theology, busted humor, and Southern survival translated into sound. The stories may be distorted by smoke, but the truth stays clean underneath.

Sounds like Eazy-E raised up in Slapout by Hank Williams Jr., then run off to Memphis with the country-rap boys coming up off the street.

This world is not fantasy. It is right beside yours. You just don't see it until life drags you close enough to the shadow.

The Warning is the first full record from Biloxi Blues Records — free, because these songs are not dressed up for a market. They are for the folks who have lived close enough to the edge to recognize the view. We don't write songs. We report from the brink.

The Band

Three Voices

Ryder McCoy

Vocals · Guitar · Songwriter

Ryder writes from the ditch side of the road. He is not trying to sound cleaned up, saved, or radio-ready. The songs come out low, plain, and bruised — like somebody telling the truth before daylight talks him out of it.

Jessie James Jenkins

Vocals

Jessie carries the smoke in the room. She brings the church weight, the hurt, and the part of the song that still wants to live. When she comes in, it stops being a story and starts sounding like somebody's name.

Unc

Vocals · Spoken Word · Backroad Noise

Unc is the old voice on the porch, half warning and half dare. He says the ugly part out loud, laughs when he should not, and reminds everybody that the road always collects what it is owed.
